Medialytics

(a basic, free analytics tool for Plex servers)

Disclaimer: This tool is not associated with Plex, and is intended only for private non-commercial use

What is Plex?

From the Plex documentation:

The Plex Media Server (PMS) is our free software that makes the magic happen behind the scenes. Once you download it onto the device where you store your files (computer or NAS device), it starts scanning and indexing your media. The PMS automatically makes your media look beautiful and intuitively organized in your Plex library, all while getting it ready for streaming.

What is Medialytics then?

This is a free tool for users who maintain a Plex server to gain more information about the content they host. Plex servers generate an XML feed of data that represents your library, such as the metadata for each library entry as well as some information about the libraries themselves. Given your server's IP address and private Plex token (do not share this value!), this script will parse your server's XML feed and render charts, statistics, and a sortable view of the content itself. The layout should work on most mobile browsers, but I haven't optimized that much. You can read more about it on github.

Why would you make this?

A couple of reasons:

  • While there are some great tools that analyze the usage of your Plex server, I couldn't find any that analyzed the content on your server.
  • Whenever I introduce Plex to someone, they want to see an example of my content in an easy to browse format. The app(s) for plex are not always the greatest for this, especially if you're using a mobile device.
  • Pulling up this Medialytics page for someone is faster than opening most apps, and there's no possibility of an embarrassing "On Deck" entry being briefly visible (if you worry about that sort of thing).
